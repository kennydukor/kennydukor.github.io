    How does Insurance work?

    Human beings are subject to risk at home, at work, on the road, while traveling, in the hospital or anywhere at any time. Insurance is a risk transfer mechanism. Insurance exists to help individuals recover from the financial consequences of unfortunate events by pooling the resources of a large group to pay for the losses of a small group. It exists because risk exists. While insurance cannot remove risks, it transfers all or some of the financial impact of any of such events to the insurer (Insurance Company). It is a practice by which a company provides a guarantee of compensation (claims) for specified loss, damage, illness or death in return for payment of a sum (premium).

    Why do I need Insurance?

    Insurance protects against financial ruin; loss of income and property and many unpredictable incidences of life. Insurance is necessary because it protects against the risks of the unknown. When you buy insurance, you transfer the cost of potential loss to the insurance company in exchange for a fee. After such a payment is made, the insurance company invests the funds securely so it can pay the policyholder when a claim is made. Some classes of insurance are required by law given the importance the government attaches to assets protection and health.

    How do I buy Insurance?

    Meet with an insurance company to talk about your coverage needs. The role of a reputable insurance company is to listen carefully to you, assess your needs and provide viable options that meet your needs and circumstances. Typically, you would execute a policy form; provide the policy requirements as well as documents such as evidence of residence and personal identification

    What is a KYC Document?

    KYC means “Know Your Customers.” It involves provision of documentation to serve as evidence of your residence, source of livelihood and identification. Consistent with the general rules and guidelines of the National Insurance Commission (NAICOM), customers are required to verify their identity and address by submitting documents that are accepted as relevant proof. Such document includes: • Proof of identity and proof of address. • Passport, • Voter's ID card, • Driving license • Utility bill e.g. electricity or water bills • Letter from an employer or any recognized public authority certifying the address.

    What is a Direct Debit Mandate?

    This is an instruction to your banker authorizing monthly deductions directly from your account for payment of your premium contributions. If you want to be on a Direct Debit, you will need to fill out a Direct Debit Mandate form. You may email customerservice@wapic.com for a copy of the Direct Debit Mandate form or download the form from our website.

    What is a Standing Order Instruction?

    A standing order (or a standing instruction) is an instruction that a bank account holder ("the payer") gives to his or her bank to pay a set amount at regular intervals to another's account ("the payee"). The instruction is sometimes known as a banker's order. At Wapic, this is currently only available to Access Bank customers. If you want this payment option, please fill out a standing instruction form at Access bank or write the branch manager of Access Bank where your account is domiciled, instructing him/her to debit your account at any period or date you want and stating that the deductions should be credited into Wapic Life Assurance Ltd Account Number: 0026787181.

    What is Sum assured?

    In insurance the sum assured is the value paid to the policy holder or dependent in case of any unforeseen circumstance.

    Who is a Policyholder?

    A policy holder is the individual or group who holds an insurance contract. A policy holder may or may not be the insured; the beneficiary can also be called a policy owner.

    Who is a Named beneficiary?

    This term refers to any beneficiary named in an insurance policy that will share in the proceeds when the policy matures. In some cases, the policyholder and the named beneficiary may be the same person.

    What is a Surrender Value?

    A surrender value is paid to a policy holder when an insurance policy is voluntarily terminated before its maturity or in event of death.

    What is a Claim?

    A claim is the payment made by the insurer (Insurance Company) to the policyholder in the event that the peril specified in the policy occurs.

    How can I make a claim?

Alternatively, refer to the policy documents given to you, to guide you. If you prefer to speak with a representative, we would be glad to talk to you.

    Who is the Customers’ Ombudsman?

    The Ombudsman of WAPIC investigates customer’s complaints and mediates fair settlement between the company and its customers.

    What does the Customers’ Ombudsman do?

    The Ombudsman receives complaints from WAPIC’s customers, regarding the company’s products and services. He investigates these complaints and settles the complaints amicably between the customer and the company.

    What does the Customers’ Ombudsman seek to achieve?

    The Ombudsman seeks to amicably resolve disputes between WAPIC and its customers by producing a win-win outcome, neither the customer nor the WAPIC losses.

    What is the philosophy of the Customers’ Ombudsman?

    The Ombudsman vision is to be the foremost complaint resolving service provider in WAPIC and the Insurance Industry in Nigeria. The mission is to provide the most suitable complaint resolutions to customers by ensuring that received complaints are promptly resolved.

    What are the principles guiding the Customers' Ombudsman in resolving complaints?

    The Ombudsman applies all the alternative dispute resolution mechanisms and the principles of natural justice in the resolution of disputes.

    Does the Customers’ Ombudsman apply laws in resolving disputes?

    No. The Ombudsman tries as much as practicable not to apply laws or legal technicalities during complaint resolution. He is only permitted to apply principles of fairness, equity and compromises in resolving complaints.

    How can the Customers’ Ombudsman be contacted?

    The Customers’ Ombudsman can be reached at WAPIC Insurance: Head Office: 119, Awolowo Road Ikoyi, Lagos. Email: complaints.ombudsman@wapic.com, Website, Social media platforms (Facebook, twitter and LinkedIn).

    Can the Customers’ Ombudsman be reached through any branch office?

    Yes. The Ombudsman can be reached through any branch of WAPIC by either filling out the complaints form and sending a scanned copy to complaints.ombudsman@wapic.com or By handing over the completed complaints form to the Customer Service Unit at any branch of WAPIC. You may also address your complaint on-line by filling out the online complaints form on our website.

    How do I know that the Customers’ Ombudsman has received my complaint?

    The Ombudsman will within 24 hours of receipt of complaint, send an acknowledgement and assure the complainant that the complaint is receiving attention.

    Can I submit my complaint over the phone?

    Yes. You may submit your complaint over telephone but you are expected to furnish sufficient details and your contact details to enable the Ombudsman revert to you.

    What happens if I do not provide all the relevant information regarding my complaint?

    The Ombudsman will call or write you to request for further information to assist in the investigation.

    What information will allow the Customers’ Ombudsman to determine whether or not my complaint can be investigated?

    The Customers’ Ombudsman needs the following information:
    1. Contact information including phone numbers
    2. Summary of the complaint
    3. Information pertaining to the loss, damage, or delay arising from WAPIC services and products if applicable
    4. Copies of all relevant documentation, including offer letter and previous correspondences, etc.
    5. Where Complaint relates to WAPIC’s products and services.

    What is the estimated duration of the investigation and mediation process employed by the Customers’ Ombudsman to settle complaints?

    On the average, the Customers’ Ombudsman will resolve a Customer’s complaint within 3 to 7 days, depending on the complexity of the complaint and number of reconciliations required to reach a fair settlement.

    How will my complaint be investigated?

    The Customers’ Ombudsman will:
    1. Interview the parties involved, as required
    2. Gather and analyze all relevant information
    3. Develop investigative plans
    4. Conduct on-site visits depending on the complexity of the complaint
    5. Issue recommendations to management of WAPIC for approval
    6. Mediate between the parties to find mutually acceptable solutions.

    How will I be informed of the outcome of the investigation?

    The Ombudsman’s finding will be communicated by letter or telephone.

    Is there a cost to use the Customers’ Ombudsman services?

    No. The Ombudsman services are free of charge to all WAPIC customers.

    Can I remain anonymous when I contact the Customers’ Ombudsman?

    No. You must provide your contact information in order for the Customers’ Ombudsman to review your complaint.

    What if I am not satisfied with the resolution reached by the Customers’ Ombudsman?

    If either party is dissatisfied with the resolution reached, the Customers’ Ombudsman may mediate further to arrive at a fair settlement. The Customers’ Ombudsman may refer the complaint to the Legal department to look into the complaint with a view to commencing legal proceedings towards resolution where necessary.

    What further steps will the Customers’ Ombudsman take when the resolution is acceptable to the parties?

    The Customers’ Ombudsman will communicate the resolution reached to both parties. Both parties are expected to within seven (7) working days to confirm their acceptance of the resolution reached. The Customers’ Ombudsman will then implement the resolutions accordingly. The Complaint file is then closed.

    What is the significance of resolving complaints through this medium?

    The Customers’ Ombudsman resolves complaints through informal means to ensure that the company/customer relationships are sustained during and after the amicably resolution of the complaint.

    Does the Customers’ Ombudsman treat the customer’s complaint confidentially?

    Yes. The Customers’ Ombudsman abides by the ethics of Insurance practice and ensures that all complaints are treated confidentially.

    How do I buy or sell Wapic GDRs?

    Investors can buy or sell DRs through their brokers like any other security.
    Brokers may source DRs through the secondary market, or may issue new DRs by instructing the purchase of ordinary shares in the local market via a local broker, and instructing them to be delivered to the account of the Depositary Bank for conversion into DR’s.
    A DR holder may return their DRs to the Depositary Bank for cancellation, at which point the local shares will be released to a specified account in the local market.
