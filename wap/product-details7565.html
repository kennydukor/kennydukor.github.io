    MOOV (Third Party)

    It is a simple insurance product which covers the policyholder against legal liability to third parties in the event of death, bodily injury and property damage involving the insured vehicle. Third party insurance is a mandatory requirement for all vehicles on Nigerian roads. It became mandatory through the Motor Vehicle (third party) Insurance Act of 1945.

    Features

    • A flat rate payable annually to insure a vehicle against all third party liabilities (subject to policy conditions)
    • The policy will still operate if you use your vehicle to tow another broken down vehicle provided you're not being paid to do so
    • Simple premium calculations

    Benefits

    • Pays third party property damage (up to a limit of N1million)
    • Pays third party property damage (up to a limit of N1million)
    • Reimbursement of legal fees if policy holder is prosecuted in court over an accident resulting in a valid claim

    To enjoy these benefits?

    • Complete the application form
    • Effect premium payment
    • Observe the terms and conditions outlined in the policy document.

    GET A QUOTE

    Documentation

    • Completed proposal form
    • Proof of ownership
    • Vehicle License
    • Acceptable proof of identification

    Exclusions and Limitations

    • Own damage, fire & theft claims are not covered
    • No provision for accidental bodily injury benefits for policyholder/driver
    • Medical expenses for anyone injured in your vehicle are excluded
    • The policy will not pay for bodily injury or damage to the property of any members of your household
    • No cover for accidents happening outside the stated geographical area (Nigeria)
    • Usual exclusions apply to war, nuclear risks and volcanic eruption
    • An excess of 10% of claim amount or N2,000 whichever is greater applies to third party property damage
    • If at the point of making a claim, it is discovered that the vehicle is also insured by other insurance companies,the cost of claim will be shared with the other companies
    • Loss of use or consequential losses

    Whole Life

    It is a life insurance policy which covers the assured for the whole of his/her life. Simply put, if the premiums have been duly paid as and when due then the sum assured is guaranteed to be paid regardless of when death occurs. It comes in two forms: ordinary whole life and limited premium payment whole life. For an ordinary whole life policy, the premiums are payable periodically till the death of the life assured; whereas for the limited premium payment option the premiums are payable till a defined age (such as 45, 50, 55, 60 or 65)

    Features

    • Premium payment pattern is flexible and can be structured according to the needs of the policyholder
    • A policyholder is entitled to a refund on part of the premium paid in the event of a Surrender
    • Flexible premium payment options (weekly, monthly, quarterly, semi-annual, annual etc.)
    • Premiums and benefits are tax deductible
    • Medical examination may be required (if sum assured exceeds a pre-defined amount)

    Benefits

    • Lump sum benefit to the family following the demise of the policyholder
    • Sum assured is guaranteed to be paid provided premiums are not outstanding
    • Cover can be terminated (or surrendered) and part of the accumulated premiums will be refunded
    • The sum assured can be reviewed upwards according to changes in lifestyle

    Claims documentation requirements

    • Medical Certificate of cause of death
    • Death Certificate (Optional)
    • Burial Certificate (if any)
    • Coroner's report (if death is by mysterious circumstances)
    • Police Report (if death is by accident)
